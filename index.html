Lok Sabha Election 2024 Report

Comprehensive insights and analysis of the recent elections.

-Total Parliamentary Constituencies

India's general election for 2024 covered 543 parliamentary constituencies, each electing one member to the Lok Sabha, the lower house of Parliament.

-Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP)

In the 2024 Indian general elections, the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) secured 240 seats, solidifying its status as the dominant political force in the Lok Sabha. This result reflects a strong performance across various states, enabling the BJP to maintain its leadership position in the central government.

-Indian National Congress (INC)

The INC secured 99 seats, coming in as the second-largest party.

-DMK's Significant Gains

Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK) won 22 seats, indicating substantial support in Tamil Nadu.

-Independent Candidates

A notable number of 7 seats were won by independent candidates.

-BJP's Sweep in Gujarat Bye-Elections

BJP won several bye-elections in Gujarat, including Porbandar, Manavadar, Khambhat, and Vaghodia.

Constituency Winning Party
Porbandar BJP
Manavadar BJP
Khambhat BJP
Vaghodia BJP

-Mixed Results in Himachal Pradesh Bye-Elections

BJP and INC both had notable wins in Himachal Pradesh, with BJP winning Dharamshala and Barsar, while INC secured Lahaul & Spiti, Sujanpur, Gagret, and Kutlehar.

-Varied Outcomes Across States

The assembly elections showed varied outcomes across different states like Arunachal Pradesh, where BJP led with 46 seats, and Andhra Pradesh, where Telugu Desam Party (TDP) won 135 seats.

State Leading Party Seats Won
Arunachal Pradesh BJP 46
Andhra Pradesh TDP 135

-Independent Candidates

Independent candidates won a total of 7 seats in the Lok Sabha elections, reflecting a diverse voter preference in certain constituencies.

- Minor Parties' Presence
